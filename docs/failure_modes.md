# Failure Modes

This document identifies how the Reflection Band discipline can fail conceptually.

Failure here is not technical malfunction.  
It is boundary erosion.

---

## 1. Helpfulness Drift

When tension-surfacing becomes advice.

If signals begin to feel “useful” in the conventional sense — by guiding, narrowing, or implying direction — the artifact has shifted from reflection to assistance.

---

## 2. Protective Reframing

When clinicians interpret the system as preventing harm.

If the artifact is perceived as catching mistakes or safeguarding outcomes, authority migration has begun.

---

## 3. Preference Creep

When ambiguity is subtly resolved into implied preference.

This can occur linguistically (“should,” “best,” “optimal”) or structurally (ranked outputs, graded urgency).

Preference creep converts reflection into instruction.

---

## 4. Vigilance Migration

When clinicians rely on the system to notice what they would otherwise monitor themselves.

Even subtle reliance weakens decisional ownership.

---

## 5. Optimization Pressure

When external stakeholders attempt to measure, score, or tune the artifact for performance improvement.

Optimization reframes the discipline as a tool.

The Reflection Band must remain constraint-first, not performance-first.

---

## Boundary Reminder

If the artifact becomes:

- predictive,
- protective,
- directive,
- or performance-evaluated,

it has drifted beyond its intended discipline.

Boundary erosion is gradual.

It must be consciously resisted.
