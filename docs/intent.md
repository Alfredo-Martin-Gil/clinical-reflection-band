# Intent

This repository documents a **thinking artifact**.

It is **not** a product, **not** a proof of concept, and **not** a clinical system.

Its purpose is to explore how AI can provide **non-competitive cognitive support** in high-pressure clinical workflows — without accelerating decisions, replacing judgment, or constraining human action.

The focus is on **where AI should speak, where it must remain silent, and why authority must always remain human**.

---

## What this is NOT

- Not a clinical decision system  
- Not triage, diagnosis, or treatment guidance  
- Not an alerting or optimization engine  
- Not workflow automation  

If it can be used to decide faster, optimize care, or enforce behavior, it is being misread.

---

## Silence as Default

Silence is not absence.

Silence is an **active signal** that no contradiction, pattern mismatch, or imminent regret has crossed a meaningful threshold.

As long as the system remains silent, the clinician can proceed knowing that nothing critical has been detected that would make the current plan unsafe.

The system speaks **rarely**, **quietly**, and **only at inflection points where reversibility is still plausible**.

---

## Reflection, not Recommendation

This artifact does not answer “what to do”.

It reflects on the clinician’s **current working hypothesis** through three restrained checks:

- Contradiction check  
- Pattern mismatch  
- Regret test  

It does not generate plans, expand differentials, or optimize outcomes.

Its role is to reduce the risk of **missing what would be hardest to regret later**.

---

## Non-Competitive Cognitive Support

The artifact must never constrain, block, or override human action.

In time-critical medicine, clinicians often accept secondary consequences deliberately to buy time.

The system’s role in those moments is **awareness, not inhibition**.

If it competes with judgment, it has failed.

---

## Authority Remains Human

All responsibility, judgment, and final action remain entirely with the clinician.

The system may warn or surface uncertainty,  
but it must never prevent action, slow execution, or introduce additional cognitive checkpoints.

If authority shifts away from the human, the design is broken.

---

## Criteria of Failure

This concept is misapplied if:

- It accelerates decision-making  
- It constrains or blocks clinical action  
- It competes with human judgment  
- It adds cognitive load  

If its impact can be measured as a standalone clinical intervention, it has already failed.
