# Example Clinical Scenario  
## Emergency Department — Chest Pain Case

This document illustrates how the **Clinical Reflection Band (CRB)** might behave during a simulated emergency department scenario.

The purpose is not to provide diagnosis or treatment guidance.  
Instead, the example demonstrates how a **reflective signal could emerge from frame instability**.

---

# Clinical Context

Environment: Emergency Department

Patient:  
Male, 58 years old

Chief complaint:  
Chest pain for 2 hours.

Initial triage:

- BP: 145/90  
- HR: 92  
- SpO₂: 97%  
- Temperature: 36.7°C  

ECG:  
Non-diagnostic.

Initial clinical impression:  
Possible non-ST elevation acute coronary syndrome.

---

# Initial Clinical Frame

The clinician constructs a working frame:

Possible diagnosis  
Acute coronary syndrome without ST elevation.

Expected trajectory

- persistent but stable pain
- stable hemodynamics
- possible troponin elevation later
- manageable with standard ACS protocol

Treatment plan

- monitoring
- laboratory tests
- analgesia
- serial ECGs

The case appears clinically plausible within this frame.

---

# Incoming Signals Over Time

30 minutes later:

Patient reports increasing discomfort.

Vital signs:

- BP: 130/85  
- HR: 105  
- SpO₂: 95%

No clear ECG changes.

---

# Frame Stability Analysis

From the clinician's perspective, the current frame remains plausible.

However, subtle signals appear:

- heart rate increasing
- blood pressure trending downward
- discomfort worsening
- patient restlessness

None of these signals alone invalidates the working diagnosis.

The workflow continues unchanged.

---

# CRB Frame Monitoring

The CRB reflective layer observes signal patterns rather than diagnoses.

Observed indicators:

- physiological trend divergence
- increasing symptom intensity
- rising sympathetic activation
- mismatch between expected stability and evolving signals

The system does not determine a diagnosis.

Instead, it evaluates **coherence between frame expectations and incoming data.**

---

# Reflection Gate Evaluation

The reflection gate evaluates whether a signal should be emitted.

Conditions considered:

- frame instability rising  
- no explicit diagnostic contradiction yet  
- clinical workflow continuing unchanged  

These conditions match the CRB trigger criteria.

---

# Reflective Signal

The system produces a minimal reflective signal.

Example signal format:

Frame instability detected

No diagnosis is suggested.

No treatment recommendation is provided.

The signal simply invites the clinician to reassess the working frame.

---

# Possible Clinical Reassessment

The clinician re-evaluates the case.

Additional considerations might emerge:

- aortic dissection  
- pulmonary embolism  
- evolving myocardial infarction  
- other causes of unstable chest pain

The reflective signal acts only as a **prompt for reconsideration**.

---

# Important Clarification

The CRB does not attempt to determine the correct diagnosis.

It simply highlights **when the current reasoning frame may no longer adequately explain the observed signals.**

---

# Design Implication

This scenario illustrates the central hypothesis of the CRB concept:

In high-pressure clinical environments, dangerous failures often occur **not because clinicians lack knowledge**, but because the **working frame remains stable while reality has begun to diverge.**

The role of reflective AI may be to detect these early instabilities before they become catastrophic.
