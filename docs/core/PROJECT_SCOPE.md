# Project Scope

## What This Repository Is

The **Clinical Reflection Band (CRB)** repository is a **conceptual system model** exploring a new category of clinical AI infrastructure: **Reflective AI**.

Unlike conventional clinical decision support systems (CDS), which attempt to generate recommendations or predictions, the CRB proposes a **Layer-2 reflective architecture** whose purpose is to monitor the **stability of the clinician’s mental model (“the clinical frame”) during evolving patient care.**

The system does **not diagnose**, **does not propose treatments**, and **does not attempt to replace clinical reasoning**.

Its sole purpose is to detect **structural tension between the clinician’s working frame and the evolving physiological reality of the patient**, and to generate **minimal reflective signals** when instability is detected.

In other words:

The CRB is not a decision engine.

It is a **frame-stability monitor for high-stakes clinical environments.**

---

## What This Repository Is NOT

This repository **is not**:

- a medical device
- a diagnostic AI system
- a clinical decision support engine
- a predictive model
- a wearable hardware product specification
- a production-ready system

No clinical deployment is implied.

The repository should be understood as a **conceptual architecture and research exploration** into safer forms of AI assistance in medicine.

---

## Intended Purpose

The CRB model is designed to stimulate exploration in three domains:

### 1. Clinical AI Safety

Many current AI systems attempt to generate answers.  
CRB explores whether **monitoring reasoning stability** might be a safer and more compatible form of AI support.

### 2. Cognitive Ergonomics in High-Pressure Medicine

Emergency medicine, prehospital care, and critical care environments impose extreme cognitive load.  
The CRB framework investigates whether **minimal reflective signals** can help clinicians detect early frame drift without interrupting workflow.

### 3. Implementation Philosophy for Clinical AI

The repository explores a different philosophy for AI integration:

- AI should **not replace authority**
- AI should **not overwhelm attention**
- AI should **not dominate decision-making**

Instead, AI may function as a **silent reflective infrastructure** operating in the background of clinical reasoning.

The CRB project is currently at a **conceptual architecture stage**.

This repository contains:

- conceptual documentation
- system-level design ideas
- exploratory reflections on clinical reasoning stability

It does **not yet include**:

- implementation prototypes
- machine learning models
- clinical datasets
- validation experiments

Future work may explore simulation frameworks or architectural prototypes.

## Guiding Principle

The central hypothesis behind the CRB model is simple:

> In high-stakes medicine, the most dangerous failures often occur **not when data is absent**, but when the **clinical frame remains stable while reality has already begun to diverge.**

The CRB proposes that AI might assist clinicians not by answering questions, but by **quietly detecting when the question itself may need to change.**
