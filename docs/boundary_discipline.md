# Boundary Discipline

This document formalizes the constraint logic underlying the Reflection Band concept.

It defines what must remain structurally protected when adaptive systems interact with human decision-makers in high-stakes, irreversible contexts.

---

## Premise

In high-uncertainty environments, the greatest risk is not informational absence but authority migration.

When a system appears helpful, clinicians may unconsciously shift vigilance upward.

Boundary discipline exists to prevent that shift.

---

## Structural Protections

1. **Ambiguity Preservation**  
   The system must not resolve ambiguity that legitimately belongs to the human decision-maker.

2. **Authority Non-Migration**  
   Decisional ownership must remain entirely human.  
   No linguistic, structural, or interactional element may imply shared authority.

3. **Epistemic Neutrality of Silence**  
   Silence must not imply validation, safety, or correctness.

4. **Non-Directive Interaction**  
   The system may surface tensions but must not recommend, rank, optimize, or guide.

5. **No Protective Framing**  
   The system must not be interpreted as preventing harm, catching errors, or safeguarding outcomes.

---

## Drift Conditions

Boundary drift occurs when:

- ambiguity becomes preference,
- tension becomes advice,
- surfacing becomes protection,
- signal becomes instruction,
- reflection becomes optimization.

Drift is conceptual, not technical.

If drift occurs, the artifact ceases to be a boundary discipline and becomes an assistive system.

---

## Integrity Test

If a clinician can say:

“The system is helping me decide,”

the boundary has weakened.

If a clinician says:

“The system surfaced something I chose to reconsider,”

the boundary remains intact.

---

## Scope

This document describes constraint logic only.

It does not describe algorithms, models, thresholds, or implementation.

Its purpose is structural clarity.
