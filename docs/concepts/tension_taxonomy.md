# Tension Taxonomy

## Purpose

The Reflection Band does not detect diagnoses, propose actions, or optimize care.

Its role is narrower:
to surface structural tensions in the current clinical frame when those tensions are perceptually meaningful under real cognitive load.

These tensions exist entirely in Layer 2 (interpretation) and must never resolve into direction or preference.

Within this taxonomy, the specific target of the **Clinical Reflection Band (CRB)** is **epistemic tension**:
the divergence between the physiological trajectory expected under the clinician’s active interpretive frame and the trajectory actually observed.

The taxonomy below describes the forms that such epistemic tension may take at the signal level.
It does not mean that the CRB is trying to detect every kind of clinical instability.

---

## Why a taxonomy is necessary

A purely generic signal such as "insufficient coherence" may be technically correct but perceptually weak.

In real environments, a reflective artifact must carry enough structural density to stabilize attention without influencing decisions.

The taxonomy below increases signal precision while remaining strictly descriptive.

---

## Tension classes

### 1. Frame mismatch

Definition  
Elements of the current presentation exceed the explanatory capacity of the working frame.

Example signal  
Stable surface. Presentation exceeds explanatory frame.

Interpretation  
The current hypothesis does not fully organize the observed data.

No action is implied.

---

### 2. Trajectory instability

Definition  
The current state appears stable but the trajectory shows emerging instability relative to what would be expected under the active frame.

Example signal  
Surface stable. Trajectory instability detected.

Interpretation  
The present snapshot is coherent, but short-term evolution may no longer match the trajectory implied by the current frame.

No direction is suggested.

---

### 3. Pattern incompleteness

Definition  
A partially recognized pattern lacks one element required for full coherence.

Example signal  
Partial pattern. One structural element missing.

Interpretation  
The system observes an incomplete alignment of known clinical structures.

No diagnostic expansion is implied.

---

### 4. Hidden-variable tension

Definition  
The observed state suggests the presence of a latent driver not currently represented in the frame.

Example signal  
Stable surface. Latent driver possible.

Interpretation  
A variable not currently captured may be shaping the trajectory.

No search strategy is implied.

---

### 5. Coherence gap

Definition  
The available data does not stabilize into a coherent frame.

Example signal  
Surface stable. Coherence gap present.

Interpretation  
The current data structure cannot yet resolve the situation into a stable interpretation.

---

## Boundary clarification

These classes describe **signal-language forms of epistemic tension**.

They are not separate operating targets competing with the core model.

The CRB does not attempt to:

- detect physiological deterioration in general
- resolve diagnostic uncertainty
- recommend diagnosis or treatment
- replace clinician judgment

Its function remains narrower:
to surface when the current interpretive frame may be losing coherence with the patient’s observed evolution.

---

## Signal discipline

Any tension signal must respect four rules:

1. No action verbs.
2. No diagnostic proposals.
3. No optimization language.
4. No preference resolution.

Signals describe state, not direction.

---

## Relation to silence

Silence remains the dominant output state.

Signals appear only when:

- state integrity is sufficient
- a structural tension is detectable
- the tension carries perceptual weight under cognitive load

If these conditions do not hold, silence is the correct response.

---

## Conceptual status

This taxonomy defines the structural vocabulary of the Reflection Band.

It is not a detection model, algorithm, or implementation.

It clarifies the forms of epistemic tension the artifact may surface without leaking into clinical authority.
