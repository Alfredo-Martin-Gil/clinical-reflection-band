# Reflection vs Clinical Decision Support

## Why this distinction matters

Many AI tools in healthcare fall into the category of Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS).

The Reflection Band is not intended to function as a decision support system.

The distinction is essential to preserve both clinical authority and conceptual clarity.

---

## What Clinical Decision Support does

Traditional decision support systems aim to:

- recommend actions
- optimize clinical pathways
- suggest diagnoses
- reduce decision variability
- guide treatment choices

These systems directly influence clinical direction.

They operate partly within the decision space of the clinician.

---

## What the Reflection Band does

The Reflection Band operates at a different layer.

It does not attempt to guide or optimize decisions.

Instead, it interacts with the **epistemic state** of the clinician during uncertain situations.

Its purpose is limited to:

- surfacing structural tensions in the current frame
- clarifying coherence gaps
- naming trajectory instability
- stabilizing attention under cognitive load

The artifact never resolves ambiguity into preference or direction.

---

## Layer interpretation

Within the three-layer model:

Layer 1 — Interaction  
Signals, timing, interface

Layer 2 — Interpretation  
Tension surfacing, coherence reflection

Layer 3 — Authority  
Clinical decisions and responsibility

The Reflection Band is intentionally restricted to **Layer 2**.

Decision authority remains entirely with the clinician.

---

## Why this matters in real environments

In high-pressure clinical environments, the problem is often not lack of knowledge but instability of perception under cognitive load.

Decision support attempts to improve decisions.

The Reflection Band attempts to stabilize the perceptual frame from which decisions emerge.

This difference is subtle but fundamental.

---

## Practical implication

A Reflection Band signal should never look like:

"Consider pulmonary embolism."

or

"Administer treatment X."

Instead, it may appear as:

"Stable surface. Coherence gap present."

or

"Surface stable. Trajectory instability detected."

These signals describe the state of the frame without influencing direction.

---

## Conceptual position

The Reflection Band can be understood as a **reflective cognitive artifact**, not a decision system.

Its value lies in preserving human authority while improving perceptual stability under uncertainty.

It does not compete with clinical reasoning.

It operates beside it.
