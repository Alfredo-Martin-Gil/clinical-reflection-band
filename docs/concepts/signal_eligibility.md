# Signal Eligibility Conditions

## Purpose

The Reflection Band operates under strict signal discipline.

Even when a structural tension exists, the artifact does not automatically emit a signal.

A signal must first satisfy eligibility conditions that ensure the reflection is perceptually meaningful and epistemically legitimate.

---

## Core principle

Not every detectable tension deserves a signal.

Signals are permitted only when the reflection can stabilize attention rather than compete with it.

---

## Eligibility conditions

A signal is eligible only when all three conditions hold.

### 1. State integrity

The observed state must be sufficiently coherent to support a meaningful reflection.

If the input state is fragmented, delayed, noisy, or incomplete, the artifact must remain silent.

This constraint is especially relevant in prehospital and public-space environments.

---

### 2. Structural tension

A genuine epistemic tension must exist within the current frame.

Examples include:

- frame mismatch
- trajectory instability
- pattern incompleteness
- hidden-variable tension
- coherence gap

If no meaningful tension exists, silence remains the correct state.

---

### 3. Perceptual relevance

The signal must carry perceptual weight under real cognitive load.

A reflection that merely restates what an experienced clinician already knows under low pressure should not appear.

Signals should reduce internal re-check loops, not introduce additional ones.

---

## Silence as default

If any eligibility condition fails, the artifact remains silent.

Silence is not absence.

Silence indicates that:

- the current frame is sufficiently coherent
- no meaningful tension is detected
- or the available state cannot support responsible reflection

---

## Field interpretation

In high-pressure environments such as emergency medicine, the cost of attention is extremely high.

For this reason, the Reflection Band follows a conservative rule:

The system must earn the right to speak.

Eligibility therefore acts as a gate before any reflective signal is allowed to appear.

---

## Conceptual status

Eligibility conditions are conceptual guardrails.

They define when reflection is epistemically legitimate and perceptually justified.

They do not imply any specific algorithm or implementation.
